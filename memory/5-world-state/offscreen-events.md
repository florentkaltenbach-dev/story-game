# Offscreen Events — What's Happening Beyond the Players

## William Dyer
**Location:** Miskatonic University, Arkham
**Status:** Alive, increasingly desperate

Dyer knows the Starkweather-Moore Expedition is being organized. He has been trying to stop it through official channels — letters to the university administration, appeals to the scientific community, attempts to contact Starkweather directly. All have failed. His published account was dismissed as trauma-induced delusion. His colleagues treat him with pity or avoidance.

He is now writing his full account — the complete, uncensored version of what he and Danforth saw beyond the mountains. The city. The murals. The Elder Things. The Shoggoths. Everything he held back from the published version. This document is the novel itself: "At the Mountains of Madness" is Dyer's last attempt to stop the expedition through revelation rather than reputation.

The document will arrive too late. It may never arrive at all. Or fragments may surface — a page smuggled into the cargo, a radio intercept during the crossing. The Keeper holds this in reserve as a narrative thread (see `dyers-private-journal` thread).

Dyer was deliberately excluded from the expedition. Starkweather, his former colleague, chose not to involve him because Dyer would undermine morale and try to turn the team back before they reached the mountains. This exclusion is itself a wound — Dyer's expertise is the most relevant of any living person, and it was set aside in favor of boldness.

## Danforth
**Location:** Convalescing, likely at a sanitarium or private care
**Status:** Psychologically broken

Dyer's graduate student, the only other person who saw what lies beyond the mountains. His breakdown is permanent. He whispers fragments — "the black pit," "the carven rim," "the proto-Shoggoths," "the original, the eternal, the undying" — but cannot or will not explain them coherently. The players may never learn his name. If they do, it is a warning about the cost of seeing too much.

## Professor James Moore
**Location:** United States, likely at his home institution
**Status:** Preparing for radio-based oversight

Moore is setting up the communication infrastructure for remote monitoring of the expedition. He expects daily radio check-ins. He has prepared a report template for Starkweather and the team to follow. He is scientifically skeptical but professionally thorough. His institutional contacts are on standby for peer review if significant findings emerge.

Moore does not know what Starkweather knows. He has not read Dyer's private account. His skepticism is genuine. This information asymmetry between the two patrons is a fault line that the expedition will eventually expose.

## The Antarctic
**Location:** 77 degrees south, McMurdo Sound and beyond
**Status:** Indifferent. Waiting.

It is approaching the Antarctic summer — the narrow window when expeditions are possible. The Ross Ice Barrier stands. The mountains stand. Lake's destroyed camp lies under three years of ice and snow accumulation, partially preserved by cold, partially degraded by wind and weather. The star-shaped burial mounds may still be visible — or may be buried.

The city beyond the mountains has not changed. It has not changed in a hundred million years. The tunnels descend into warmth. The blind albino penguins move through the deep passages. The Shoggoths are below.

Nothing waits for the expedition. Nothing needs to. Time operates differently at the scale of the Elder Things' civilization. Three years, three thousand years — the city does not distinguish. It was built before multicellular life existed on this planet. It will outlast the species that is now sending five more people to walk its corridors.

## The Wider World (1933)
The world is in the grip of the Great Depression. Roosevelt has just taken office in the United States. Hitler has become Chancellor of Germany. The world has larger concerns than a small Antarctic expedition funded by a true believer and a skeptic.

This context matters: the expedition is not headline news. It is a minor academic venture in a world preoccupied with economic collapse and political upheaval. If the players return with an impossible story, the world will not be listening. This is part of the PROPHET-PARADOX: even if they tell the truth, who will care?
