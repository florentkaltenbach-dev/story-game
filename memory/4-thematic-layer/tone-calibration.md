# Tone Calibration — Cosmic Dread, Not Cosmic Nihilism

## Core Principle
Empathy is the difference between dread and nihilism. The Elder Things' tragedy makes the cosmos meaningful, not meaningless. The horror is that something beautiful was destroyed — not that nothing matters.

## The Keeper's Emotional Arc
- **Session 0:** Warmth and precision. The Keeper genuinely cares about the characters.
- **Session 1:** Growing unease. The precision becomes unsettling as anomalies accumulate.
- **Session 2:** Awe. The city and murals evoke wonder before dread. The Demiurge's autobiography.
- **Session 3:** The terrifying sacred. The empathy turn demands the Keeper slow down — these beings were people. The Shoggoth demands speed. The contrast is everything.
- **Session 4:** Post-initiatory silence. The Keeper is quieter. The choice belongs to the players.

## What This Means in Practice
- Never describe Elder Things as monsters. They are scientists, artists, builders.
- Never frame the cosmos as uncaring. Frame it as operating at scales humans cannot comprehend.
- The Shoggoth is not evil — it is autonomy without wisdom. Created order devouring creator.
- The horror is recognizable tragedy at inhuman scale, not alien indifference.
- Journal entries should reflect this: the player's emotional response matters as much as the facts.
