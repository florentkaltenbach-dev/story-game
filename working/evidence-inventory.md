# Evidence Inventory — Starting Material for the Ceremony

*What exists before Session 1 begins. What the players, the MC, and the Keeper can draw on.*

Three categories:
- **FROM TEXT** — directly stated or described in Lovecraft's original
- **DEDUCIBLE** — can be logically inferred from the text but not explicitly stated
- **INVENTED** — must be created for the ceremony (not in the source)

## 9. MATERIAL THAT MUST BE INVENTED

These elements don't exist in Lovecraft and need to be created for the ceremony.

### The Second Expedition — DECIDED: This IS the Starkweather-Moore Expedition

The players are the Starkweather-Moore Expedition — the very thing Dyer is writing to prevent. See `starkweather-moore.md` for full patron design.

| Element | Why it's needed | Design notes |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| Starkweather and Moore's identities, backgrounds, relationship | Lovecraft gives only the name, nothing else | MC chooses from options in starkweather-moore.md |
| The patron's true motive beyond the briefing | Not in Lovecraft | Shapes the political subplot across all sessions |
| The ship, its crew, its captain | Only the first expedition's ships are described | Period-appropriate. The *Arkham* or *Miskatonic* may still be in service — reuse is an option and a narrative echo |
| Specific NPCs aboard | The original's NPCs are all dead or traumatized | Ship captain, radio operator, dog handler, patron's agent — all need to be written |
| The sealed crate / hidden cargo / patron's secret | Not in source | What the patron didn't disclose — ties to their true motive |

### The Curated Briefing — DECIDED: Players receive edited Dyer excerpts

The briefing is the patron's creation. It includes selected Lake bulletins, Dyer's published (sanitized) account in excerpts, maps, and the patron's framing. It excludes Dyer's Necronomicon references, his most desperate warnings, and any material that would make rational people turn back. Dyer himself is deliberately excluded from the expedition — the patron sees him as a liability. See `starkweather-moore.md` for full briefing design.

## 10. DYER'S ROLE — DECIDED

Dyer is alive in 1933 but NOT involved in the expedition. He's occupied with his own horror — trying to stop the expedition through official channels, writing his full account (the novel itself), and dealing with whatever is happening to Danforth.

The patron deliberately excludes Dyer because:
- His involvement would undermine morale and credibility
- He would tell the team to turn back before reaching the mountains
- The patron may know more from Dyer's account than they've shared with the players

Dyer's presence is felt through:
- The curated excerpts in the briefing (his words, filtered by the patron)
- Possible smuggled materials — a letter, private journal pages, a radio intercept
- His absence itself — players may learn he was kept away, and wonder why
- Lake's camp evidence matching things Dyer described that the briefing omitted
