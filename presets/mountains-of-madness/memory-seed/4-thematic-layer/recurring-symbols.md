# Recurring Symbols — Tracking Across Sessions

## The Five-Pointed Star
The Elder Thing motif. Appears in: soapstone fragments (Session 1), architectural geometry (Session 2), floor markings at tunnel entrance (Session 3). The Keeper seeds this incrementally — players should recognize the pattern before they understand its origin.

**Current status:** Not yet planted. First appearance at Lake's camp (Session 1 Act III).

## The Piping Wind
Azathoth resonance. Begins as atmospheric detail (ship rigging, Session 1 Act I), crescendos through cave mouths (Session 2), and is finally recognized as non-wind in Session 3. The Keeper describes it differently each time — always musical, always almost-meaningful.

**Current status:** Not yet planted.

## Dark-Green Ichor
The Elder Thing body fluid. First encountered at Lake's camp as residue (Session 1), then as pooling liquid near headless bodies (Session 3). The color is specific: not green, not black — dark-green, thick, with an organic pungency.

**Current status:** Not yet planted.

## Dot-Group Writing
The Elder Thing notation system. Visible on soapstone, walls, instruments. The players can see it is writing but cannot read it. The system is mathematical — dot clusters in geometric arrangements. The Keeper describes it precisely enough that players feel the weight of an unreadable language.

**Current status:** Not yet planted.

## The Crude Carvings
Shoggoth imitation art overlaid on Elder Thing originals. A palimpsest. The style degrades — the proportions are wrong, the technique is mimicry without understanding. This is the Ahrimanic principle made visible: the created thing attempting the creator's art.

**Current status:** Not yet planted. First noticed in city murals (Session 2 Act II).
