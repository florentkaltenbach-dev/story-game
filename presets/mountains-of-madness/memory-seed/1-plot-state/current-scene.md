# Current Scene — The Arkham Wharf

## Location
Arkham, Massachusetts. The university wharf on the Miskatonic River, where it meets the harbor. Late autumn, 1933.

## Setting
The expedition vessel is moored at the dock. Crates of equipment line the quay — drilling apparatus, radio sets, canned provisions, cold-weather gear, cages for the sledge dogs. Longshoremen move cargo under electric lights that catch the river fog. The smell of brine, engine oil, and wet rope. Somewhere upriver, the Miskatonic University library is lit. Somewhere in that library, William Dyer is writing a document that will arrive too late.

The wharf is the last civilized ground. After this, the world narrows — the Atlantic, the Southern Ocean, the Ross Sea, the ice. Everything that follows begins here, under these lamps, in this cold New England air.

## Dramatic Purpose
This is the gathering. Characters meet, size each other up, receive the patron's briefing, and make their departure statements. The scene is entirely Stratum I — mundane, rational, human. The only wrongness present is human wrongness: edited truths, conflicting motives, the patron's hidden agenda, and the fact that every person here is ignoring the warning of a man they've chosen not to invite.

## Tone
Clinical. Rational. Warm. The Keeper's register is welcoming and precise. The horror is patient — it is not present yet. What is present is the ordinary courage of people choosing to go somewhere dangerous, and the ordinary compromises of people who want different things from the same journey.
