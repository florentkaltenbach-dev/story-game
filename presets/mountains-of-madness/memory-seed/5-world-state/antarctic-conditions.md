# Antarctic Conditions — Pre-Expedition

## Season
Approaching Antarctic summer (November-February). The narrow operational window. Daylight is nearly continuous at high latitudes.

## McMurdo Sound Area
- Open water accessible from late December through February in most years.
- Ross Ice Barrier stands at approximately 200 feet vertical. The edge of the navigable south.
- Temperatures at base camp: -10C to -30C depending on wind and cloud cover.
- Wind is the primary hazard — katabatic gusts can reach hurricane force with no warning.

## The Interior
- The plateau is 8,500+ feet above sea level. Thin air, extreme cold, featureless ice.
- Beardmore Glacier is the crossing point from coast to interior. Crevasse fields are lethal.
- The mountain range (Lovecraft's unnamed peaks) exceeds 35,000 feet — higher than Everest. Unclimbed, uncharted, bare black rock.

## Lake's Camp (1930 remains)
- Three years of ice accumulation over the destroyed advance camp.
- Star-shaped burial mounds may be partially visible or fully buried.
- Specimen containers, dissection tools, and Lake's field journal are likely preserved by cold.
- Gedney's body was never found.

## The City Beyond
- Cyclopean architecture extending along the mountain foothills. Scale: visible from the air.
- Tunnel entrance(s) at city level — fifteen-foot carved passages descending into anomalous warmth.
- Underground ecosystem: giant blind albino penguins, warm air, the nameless scent.
- The Shoggoths are below.
