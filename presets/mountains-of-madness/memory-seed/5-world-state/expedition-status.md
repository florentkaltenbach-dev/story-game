# Expedition Status — Session 0, Pre-Departure

## Overview
The Starkweather-Moore Expedition is in its final stage of preparation at Arkham, Massachusetts. The expedition vessel is moored at the university wharf on the Miskatonic River. Departure is imminent.

## Patron Configuration (Option B)
- **Dr. Harold Starkweather** — aboard the vessel. True believer. Has read Dyer's private account. Motive: vindication.
- **Professor James Moore** — remains in the United States. Radio contact only. Skeptic. Motive: debunking or rigorous claim. His name gives the expedition institutional credibility.

## Expedition Resources
- **Vessel:** Moored at Arkham wharf, provisioned for the Southern Ocean crossing and Antarctic operations
- **Equipment:** Drilling apparatus, Dornier-type seaplanes, radio sets (shortwave for long-range contact with Moore), cold-weather gear, photographic equipment, geological survey tools
- **Dogs:** Alaskan sledge dogs, caged aboard the vessel
- **Crew:** Ship captain (NPC, to be established), radio operator (NPC), dog handler (NPC), plus expedition members (the players)
- **Provisions:** Sufficient for estimated 4-6 months of Antarctic operations

## The Briefing
The patron's curated briefing has been prepared. It contains:
- Dyer's published account (sanitized excerpts — the "camp disaster" narrative)
- Selected Lake bulletins (geological findings, anomalous fossils, enough to establish scientific interest)
- Aerial photographs and maps from the first expedition confirming the mountain range
- Newspaper coverage from 1931
- The patron's own analysis framing the expedition as a follow-up geological survey

It excludes:
- Dyer's references to the Necronomicon, Elder Things, and the Cthulhu cult
- Lake's most frantic bulletins
- Any mention of Dyer's plea to stop this specific expedition
- Danforth's condition (players may not know he exists)

## Route Plan
1. Arkham wharf to the Southern Ocean
2. Southern Ocean to McMurdo Sound (Ross Sea)
3. Establish base camp near McMurdo Sound
4. Inland surveys, retracing elements of the first expedition's route
5. Approach and investigation of the mountain range confirmed by aerial photography
6. The patron's orders: cross the mountains

## Current Phase
**Gathering.** Characters are arriving, meeting each other, receiving the briefing, establishing relationships and tensions. No operations have begun. The expedition exists as intention, not yet as action.
